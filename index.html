Self-Care Tips

Small actions, meaningful change.

🌬️

Box Breathing

Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times. This activates your parasympathetic nervous system and reduces acute anxiety within minutes.

🚶

Move Your Body

Even a 10-minute walk changes your brain chemistry. Step outside, notice your surroundings, and let movement break the cycle of rumination.

💧

Hydrate First

Dehydration amplifies feelings of anxiety and fatigue. Before reaching for your phone in the morning, drink a full glass of water.

📵

Digital Sunset

Stop screens 60 minutes before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin; replacing that time with a book or gentle stretching measurably improves sleep quality.

✍️

Three Good Things

Each evening, write down three specific things that went well today, however small. This rewires attention toward the positive without toxic positivity.

🧹

Tidy One Thing

Clutter is a low-grade stressor. Pick one surface, drawer, or corner and clear it. Completing a visible task creates a concrete sense of control.

🫂

Reach Out

Send a text to one person you haven't spoken to in a while. Connection is protective; you don't need to share how you're feeling — just saying hello is enough.

😴

Guard Your Sleep

Sleep is not a luxury — it is the foundation every other coping strategy rests on. Set a consistent bedtime and treat it like a non-negotiable appointment.
