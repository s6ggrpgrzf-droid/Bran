    Self-Care at Work

    Small resets, right at your desk.

    🌬️

    Desk Breathing

    Feeling overwhelmed mid-task? Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Two quiet rounds at your desk is enough to drop your stress response and clear your head.

    🚶

    Take a Walk Break

    Step away from your screen for 5–10 minutes — down the hall, outside, anywhere. Movement breaks the tension loop and you come back with a clearer perspective.

    💧

    Keep Water Nearby

    Dehydration quietly amplifies stress and brain fog. Keep a water bottle at your desk and take a sip whenever you switch tasks — it's a tiny reset that adds up.

    🤸

    Stretch It Out

    Tension hides in your neck, shoulders, and jaw. Roll your shoulders back, tilt your head side to side, and unclench your jaw — you can do it all without leaving your chair.

    Show 4 more tips
    📋

    Brain Dump

    When your mind is spinning with tasks, write every single thing down — sticky note, app, anything. Getting it out of your head and onto paper stops the loop and lets you focus.

    🧹

    Clear Your Desk

    A cluttered workspace is a background stressor. Take two minutes to clear what's in front of you. A tidy surface signals your brain that things are under control.

    🫂

    Connect With Someone

    A quick hello to a coworker or a short text to a friend can shift your whole mood. You don't need to talk about anything heavy — connection itself is the reset.

    ✍️

    Three Wins at Lunch

    At lunch, jot down three things that went well this morning — a task finished, a good interaction, anything. Noticing progress rewires how your afternoon feels before it starts.
