Who She Was

Tracy Willis was the kind of woman who made a room feel different the moment she walked into it — warmer, louder, more alive. Born in Lubbock, Texas, she answered her country's call in 1987 at Fort McClellan, and went on to serve in the Oklahoma Army National Guard, deploying multiple times with the 45th Transportation Unit and leading as a squad leader before retiring with every honor she'd earned.

She spent twenty more years as a correctional officer with the Oklahoma Department of Corrections — a supervisor, a mentor, the standard everyone else measured themselves against. She was meticulous with her uniform and commanded every room she stood in. She retired from Mabel Bassett in 2024, after giving 37 years of herself to service.

But what no record captures is the laugh. The spotless white Nikes. The coffee that was permanently in her hand. The way everything she wore was just right — classy, fancy, always her. The way she loved the beach and the way sunlight made her feel free. The way she adored Cholly and Nacho. The way she loved Brandon and Paris more than anything this world has to offer.

For You, Brandon

She's not gone — not really. She's in the way you laugh too loud in a quiet room, just like she did. She's in the way you show up for the people you love, every single time, the way she showed up for you every single day of your life.

She raised someone worth being proud of, and she was. She always was. Grief is just love with nowhere left to go — so let it remind you how much she gave, and keep giving like she did. Classy. Full of heart. Never without a smile.

When the waves come and the weight gets heavy, picture her: sunglasses on, coffee in hand, white Nikes spotless, grinning like she knows something you don't. Because she did. She always did. 💜

💜   Rest gently, Tracy. The waves are always yours.   💜
